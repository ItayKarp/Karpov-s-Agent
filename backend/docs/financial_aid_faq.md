# Financial Aid FAQ — Westbrook University

## General Questions

### What types of financial aid are available?
Westbrook offers several forms of financial aid:
- **Merit Scholarships** — awarded based on academic achievement
- **Need-Based Grants** — awarded based on financial need (no repayment required)
- **Federal Loans** — subsidized and unsubsidized (must be repaid)
- **Work-Study** — part-time on-campus employment funded by federal program
- **Private/External Scholarships** — from outside organizations; can be applied to your account

**Questions this section answers:** What types of financial aid are available? What is the difference between grants and loans? Is there merit-based aid? Is there need-based aid? Does Westbrook offer scholarships?

### How do I apply for financial aid?
1. Complete the **FAFSA** (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) at studentaid.gov using Westbrook's school code: **003421**.
2. Submit any additional documents requested by the Financial Aid Office (ADM 120).
3. Review your Financial Aid Award Letter in the student portal.
4. Accept or decline individual aid components by the deadline shown in your portal.

## Scholarships

### Westbrook Merit Scholarship
- **Award:** $5,000–$15,000 per academic year
- **Eligibility:** Incoming freshmen with GPA ≥ 3.7 and SAT ≥ 1350 (or ACT ≥ 29)
- **Renewal:** Maintain cumulative GPA ≥ 3.5 each academic year
- **Application:** Automatic consideration upon admission; no separate application required

**Questions this section answers:** How much is the merit scholarship? What GPA do I need for the merit scholarship? What SAT score do I need for the merit scholarship? Is the merit scholarship automatic? How do I keep my merit scholarship?

### Presidential Excellence Award
- **Award:** Full tuition for 4 years
- **Eligibility:** Top 10 admitted students per class; holistic review of GPA, leadership, essays
- **Application Deadline:** January 15 (same as early decision deadline)

**Questions this section answers:** What is the Presidential Excellence Award? Is there a full tuition scholarship? How do I apply for the Presidential Excellence Award? What is the deadline for the Presidential Excellence Award?

### STEM Achievement Grant
- **Award:** $8,000/year
- **Eligibility:** Declared major in CS, Math, Biology, Chemistry, or Physics; GPA ≥ 3.3
- **Application Deadline:** March 1 for incoming students; April 15 for continuing students
- **Contact:** stemgrant@westbrook.edu

**Questions this section answers:** Is there a STEM scholarship? How much is the STEM grant? What GPA do I need for the STEM grant? When is the STEM grant deadline? What majors qualify for the STEM grant?

### Need-Based Westbrook Grant
- **Award:** $2,000–$20,000/year based on demonstrated need
- **Eligibility:** FAFSA required; Expected Family Contribution (EFC) determines amount
- **Renewal:** Submit FAFSA annually by March 1; maintain satisfactory academic progress

**Questions this section answers:** Is there need-based financial aid? How much is the need-based grant? How do I get a need-based grant? Do I need to reapply for grants every year?

### Transfer Student Scholarship
- **Award:** $4,000–$10,000/year
- **Eligibility:** Transfer students with college GPA ≥ 3.0 and ≥ 30 transferable credits
- **Application Deadline:** May 1 for Fall; November 1 for Spring

**Questions this section answers:** Is there a scholarship for transfer students? How much is the transfer scholarship? What GPA do I need for the transfer scholarship?

## Loans

### Federal Subsidized Loans
- **Interest:** Government pays interest while you're enrolled at least half-time
- **Annual limit:** $3,500 (freshman), $4,500 (sophomore), $5,500 (junior/senior)
- **Repayment:** Begins 6 months after graduation or dropping below half-time

### Federal Unsubsidized Loans
- **Interest:** Accrues immediately from disbursement
- **Annual limit:** $2,000 additional beyond subsidized limits
- **Note:** Interest can be paid during enrollment to reduce total debt

### Parent PLUS Loans
- Available for parents of dependent undergraduate students
- Requires separate credit check; apply at studentaid.gov

## Work-Study

- **Eligibility:** Must demonstrate financial need via FAFSA
- **Hours:** Maximum 20 hours/week during the academic year
- **Pay:** $13–$16/hour depending on position
- **Jobs:** Library, dining, administrative offices, IT help desk, tutoring center
- **How to find positions:** studentjobs.westbrook.edu (log in with student credentials)

## Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

To remain eligible for financial aid, students must:
- Maintain a cumulative GPA ≥ 2.0
- Complete at least 67% of attempted credits each semester
- Finish degree within 150% of the standard program length (e.g., 6 years for a 4-year degree)

Students who fail SAP are notified by the Financial Aid Office and may appeal if there were extenuating circumstances.
