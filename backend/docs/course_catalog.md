# Course Catalog — Westbrook University (2025–2026)

## College of Arts & Sciences

### CS101 — Introduction to Computer Science
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Fundamentals of programming using Python. Topics include variables, control flow, functions, and basic data structures. Ideal for non-majors and first-year CS students.

### CS201 — Data Structures & Algorithms
- **Credits:** 4
- **Prerequisites:** CS101
- **Description:** Arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs, sorting, and searching algorithms. Emphasis on time/space complexity analysis.

### CS301 — Operating Systems
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** CS201
- **Description:** Process management, memory management, file systems, and concurrency. Includes a systems programming lab in C.

### CS401 — Machine Learning
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** CS201, MATH202
- **Description:** Supervised and unsupervised learning, neural networks, model evaluation. Hands-on projects using scikit-learn and PyTorch.

### MATH101 — Calculus I
- **Credits:** 4
- **Prerequisites:** High school algebra (placement test)
- **Description:** Limits, derivatives, and integrals of single-variable functions. Applications in physics and economics.

### MATH202 — Linear Algebra & Probability
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** MATH101
- **Description:** Vectors, matrices, eigenvalues, probability distributions, and statistics. Essential for CS and engineering majors.

### ENG110 — Academic Writing
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Develops critical reading, argumentation, and essay writing skills. Required for all undergraduates.

### BIO150 — Introduction to Biology
- **Credits:** 4 (includes lab)
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Cell biology, genetics, evolution, and ecology. Lab component meets weekly for 2 hours.

### PHYS101 — General Physics I
- **Credits:** 4 (includes lab)
- **Prerequisites:** MATH101 (may be co-requisite)
- **Description:** Mechanics, kinematics, Newton's laws, energy, and momentum. Weekly lab sessions.

### PSY101 — Introduction to Psychology
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Survey of human behavior and mental processes. Topics include perception, cognition, emotion, and social influence.

## College of Business

### BUS101 — Principles of Management
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Organizational theory, leadership styles, decision-making, and business ethics.

### BUS210 — Financial Accounting
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Balance sheets, income statements, cash flow analysis, and GAAP fundamentals.

### BUS320 — Marketing Strategy
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** BUS101
- **Description:** Market segmentation, branding, digital marketing, and consumer behavior.

### ECON101 — Microeconomics
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Supply and demand, market structures, consumer theory, and welfare economics.

## General Education Requirements

All students must complete:
- **ENG110** — Academic Writing
- 1 course from Quantitative Reasoning (MATH or STAT prefix)
- 1 course from Natural Sciences (BIO, CHEM, PHYS prefix)
- 1 course from Social Sciences (PSY, SOC, ECON prefix)
- 1 course from Humanities (HIST, PHIL, LIT prefix)
- 2 electives from any college

## Credit Load Policy
- Full-time: 12–18 credits per semester
- Overload (>18 credits): requires advisor approval
- Minimum for financial aid: 12 credits
