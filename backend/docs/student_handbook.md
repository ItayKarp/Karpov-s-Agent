# Student Handbook — Westbrook University

## Academic Policies

### Grading Scale

| Grade | GPA Points | Percentage |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| A+ | 4.0 | 97–100% |
| A | 4.0 | 93–96% |
| A− | 3.7 | 90–92% |
| B+ | 3.3 | 87–89% |
| B | 3.0 | 83–86% |
| B− | 2.7 | 80–82% |
| C+ | 2.3 | 77–79% |
| C | 2.0 | 73–76% |
| C− | 1.7 | 70–72% |
| D | 1.0 | 60–69% |
| F | 0.0 | Below 60% |

Special grades:
- **W** — Withdrawal (no GPA impact; appears on transcript)
- **I** — Incomplete (must be resolved within 6 weeks of semester end; converts to F otherwise)
- **P/F** — Pass/Fail option available for electives only; requires form submitted by Week 2

### GPA Requirements
- **Good Standing:** GPA ≥ 2.0
- **Dean's List:** Semester GPA ≥ 3.5, full-time enrollment (≥12 credits)
- **Academic Probation:** GPA falls below 2.0 for one semester → advising required
- **Academic Suspension:** GPA below 2.0 for two consecutive semesters → suspension for one semester
- **Dismissal:** GPA below 2.0 for three cumulative semesters

## Attendance Policy

- Regular attendance is expected in all courses.
- Instructors set their own attendance policies; refer to each course syllabus.
- After **3 unexcused absences**, an instructor may lower your grade or report you to Academic Affairs.
- **Excused absences:** Illness (with documentation), bereavement, religious observance, official university events. Submit documentation to your instructor within 5 business days.
- **Extended illness:** Contact the Dean of Students Office (SU 210) to coordinate official notifications to all instructors.

## Academic Integrity & Honor Code

Westbrook University expects all students to uphold the highest standards of academic honesty.

### Prohibited Conduct
- **Cheating:** Using unauthorized materials during exams or assessments.
- **Plagiarism:** Submitting someone else's work as your own without proper citation.
- **Fabrication:** Inventing data, sources, or experimental results.
- **Unauthorized Collaboration:** Working with others on assignments designated as individual work.
- **Facilitation:** Helping another student violate the Honor Code.
- **AI Misuse:** Submitting AI-generated text as your own where prohibited by course policy.

### Reporting & Consequences
1. Instructor reports suspected violation to Academic Integrity Office (ADM 200).
2. Student is notified and given opportunity to respond.
3. Hearing panel convenes within 15 business days.
4. Possible sanctions: warning, grade penalty, course failure, suspension, or expulsion.
5. Students may appeal sanctions within 10 business days of decision.

## Code of Student Conduct

### Behavioral Expectations
- Treat all members of the campus community with respect.
- No harassment, discrimination, or hate speech of any kind.
- No physical altercations or threats of violence.
- No vandalism or damage to university property.
- Alcohol is prohibited for students under 21; no alcohol in dry residence halls.
- Illegal drugs and weapons are strictly prohibited on campus.

### Reporting Misconduct
- Contact the Dean of Students Office: SU 210, (555) 200-2000
- Anonymous reporting: westbrook.edu/safereport
- Emergency situations: Campus Police at (555) 911-0000 or dial 911
