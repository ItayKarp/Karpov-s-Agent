# Registration FAQ — Westbrook University

## Getting Started

### When can I register?
Registration windows are assigned based on credit hours earned (not including in-progress credits):

| Credits Earned | Registration Opens |
|---------------|-------------------|
| 90+ (Senior) | First day of priority registration |
| 60–89 (Junior) | +1 business day after seniors |
| 30–59 (Sophomore) | +2 business days after seniors |
| 0–29 (Freshman) | +3 business days after seniors |

Check your exact registration date and time in the student portal under **My Registration**.

**Questions this section answers:** When can I register for classes? When does registration open? Does registration depend on credits? When do seniors register? When do freshmen register? How do I find my registration date?

### How do I register for classes?
1. Log in to the student portal at my.westbrook.edu.
2. Navigate to **Registration → Course Search & Register**.
3. Search by course number, title, instructor, or time.
4. Add courses to your **cart** (does not enroll you yet).
5. At your registration window, submit your cart.
6. Check your schedule under **My Schedule** to confirm enrollment.

**Questions this section answers:** How do I register for classes? How do I enroll in a course? Where do I sign up for classes? How do I use the student portal to register? How do I confirm my enrollment?

### Do I need to see my advisor before registering?
- **New freshmen and transfer students:** Yes — mandatory advising hold is placed; you must meet with your advisor before the hold is lifted.
- **All other students:** Strongly recommended but not required. Advising hold may be placed if you are on academic probation.
- Book advisor appointments at: advising.westbrook.edu (ALD 115, Mon–Fri 9 AM – 4 PM)

**Questions this section answers:** Do I need to see an advisor before registering? Is advising required? What is an advising hold? Where do I book an advising appointment? Do transfer students need to see an advisor?

## Adding & Dropping Courses

### What is the last day to add a course?
- **Without instructor signature:** End of the first week of classes (see academic_calendar.md for exact date)
- **With instructor signature:** End of the second week of classes
- After Week 2: Adding a course requires approval from the instructor, department chair, and Registrar.

### What is the last day to drop a course?
- **Full refund, no record on transcript:** End of Week 2
- **W grade (no GPA impact, appears on transcript):** Until Week 10 (see academic_calendar.md for exact date)
- **After Week 10:** Drops require dean's approval and are only granted for extraordinary circumstances (medical emergency, family crisis with documentation).

### How do I add or drop a course?
- **Online (during open add/drop):** my.westbrook.edu → Registration → Add/Drop
- **Paper form:** Available at the Registrar's Office (ADM 110); requires signatures based on timing

### What happens to tuition if I drop a course?
| Drop Timing | Refund |
|-------------|--------|
| Before classes begin | 100% |
| Week 1–2 | 100% |
| Week 3 | 75% |
| Week 4 | 50% |
| Week 5 | 25% |
| Week 6+ | No refund |

## Waitlist Policy

### How does the waitlist work?
- If a course is full, you can add yourself to the waitlist via the student portal.
- If a seat opens (due to a drop), the next person on the waitlist is **automatically enrolled** and notified by email.
- You have **24 hours** to accept or decline the seat; if you do not respond, you are removed from the waitlist.
- You are responsible for monitoring your email during add/drop period.

### Can I attend a class I'm on the waitlist for?
- Yes, attend while on the waitlist — you will not be penalized if a seat never opens.
- Instructors are not required to accommodate waitlisted students for exams or assignments unless enrolled.
- Ask the instructor for permission to attend; most will allow it.

### Maximum waitlist position:
- You can be on a waitlist for up to **3 courses** simultaneously.
- Waitlists close at the end of Week 2; after that, you need direct instructor/department permission.

## Course Conflicts & Overrides

### Prerequisite override
- If you believe you have the equivalent background for a course despite not meeting the listed prerequisite:
  1. Contact the instructor with evidence of equivalent preparation.
  2. If approved, the instructor submits an override to the Registrar.
  3. You can then register for the course.

### Time conflict override
- Courses with overlapping meeting times cannot be added without a time conflict override.
- Both instructors must approve and submit override forms.
- Only granted in exceptional circumstances — contact the Registrar's Office (ADM 110).

### Section override (closed section)
- If a section is full and you have a compelling reason to be in that specific section:
  - Contact the instructor for a permission number (a code entered during registration).

## Graduation Requirements

### General Requirements (Bachelor's Degree)
- **Minimum credits:** 120 credits
- **Minimum GPA:** 2.0 cumulative; many majors require 2.5+ in major courses
- **Residency requirement:** At least 30 of the final 36 credits must be taken at Westbrook
- **General Education:** Complete all Gen Ed requirements (see student_handbook.md)
- **Major requirements:** Declared major courses must be completed with C or better
- **Application to graduate:** Must be submitted 2 semesters before your expected graduation date

### Applying to Graduate
1. Log in to my.westbrook.edu → Graduation → Apply to Graduate
2. Submit by the deadline:
   - **May graduation:** Apply by October 1 of the preceding fall
   - **December graduation:** Apply by March 1 of the preceding spring
3. Meet with your advisor to review your Degree Audit (also available in the portal).
4. Receive confirmation from the Registrar within 4–6 weeks.

### Degree Audit
- View your real-time progress toward graduation at my.westbrook.edu → Degree Audit.
- Shows completed, in-progress, and remaining requirements for your major and Gen Ed.
- Discrepancies? Contact the Registrar (ADM 110) or your academic advisor (ALD 115).

## Transferring Credits

### Transfer Credit Evaluation
- Credits from accredited institutions may transfer if the course is comparable to a WU course.
- Submit official transcripts to the Registrar's Office (ADM 110).
- Course-by-course evaluation: 4–6 weeks; check status in the student portal.
- Accepted as: direct equivalents, elective credits, or Gen Ed fulfillments.
- Maximum transfer credits: 60 credits from 2-year institutions; 90 from 4-year institutions.

### AP / IB Credits
- AP: Score of 4 or 5 generally earns credit; see westbrook.edu/ap-credit for the chart.
- IB: Higher Level scores of 5+ generally earn credit; see westbrook.edu/ib-credit.
- Submit official AP/IB scores to the Registrar.
